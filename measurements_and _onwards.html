em is 16 px. font-size: 2em; (2 * 16px = 32px)

em size varies according to it's parent font-size. .em-parent{font-size:10px} and #em-child{font-size: 2em;}. So, the font-size of em-child will be 2 * 10px = 20px. Since font-size of its parent is 10px

Rem

rem size varies according to the root element's font-size. That is <html>. Generally, html size is 16px. #rem-child{ font-size: 2rem; } so it will be 2 * 16px = 32px;

Margin-top will be 24px, as the font-size of the element is 12px. And the 2em margin-top will result in 2 * font-size of the element = 24px;

Margin-top will be 32px. The font-size property will not effect rem. 2rem margin-top will result in 2 * 16px = 32px;
